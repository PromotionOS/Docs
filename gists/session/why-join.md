# Why You Should Join — Claude Done Right

## For Tech Leads Who Already Use Claude

You already use Claude. You have used it to write code, debug issues, explain things, draft documents. It is useful. You know that.

But here is the question: **are the engineers on your team using it right?**

More importantly: **are you?**

This workshop is not for people who have never used Claude. It is for tech leads who use it every day and want to understand what the ceiling actually looks like — and how to bring their teams up to that ceiling.

---

## What You Will Experience

You will own one service in a real, distributed system. You work alone. Claude is your team.

By the end of 4 sessions you will have:

- Built a production-grade service from scratch using Spec Driven Development
- Experienced Domain Driven Design not as a concept but as something you feel when the code stops fighting the requirements
- Communicated across service boundaries with other TLs using contract skills — no Slack, no meetings
- Validated your implementation against real-world retail data
- Shipped something that runs on Railway and talks to 5 other live services

## The System You Are Building

**PromotionOS** — a trade promotions management platform.

Oracle charges retail companies like Kroger $500,000 a year for this. You are building the core of it. In 8 hours.

Not a toy version. Not a tutorial. A real system with:
- Domain Driven Design — bounded contexts, aggregates, value objects, domain events
- Event-driven architecture — Redis Pub/Sub, 8 domain event channels
- Multi-service integration — 6 services talking to each other through contracts
- Real test data — 12 campaigns, 12 customers, 48 UPCs, 30 validation scenarios

## What Makes This Different

**You work with real constraints.**

Your service has a bug planted in it. You will find it using a root cause analysis. Fix it through the SDD process. Document it. The process is the point — not the fix.

**You communicate through skills, not Slack.**

When the Catalog TL changes their API contract, they update a contract skill. You use that skill to understand the blast radius and refactor your anti-corruption layer. No coordination meeting. No ticket. The skill is the handoff.

**The Frontend TL wires to your live API from Session 1.**

Not mocks. Not stubs. The moment your service deploys, the frontend is hitting it. That is real integration pressure. That is what it feels like to own a service in a real org.

**You leave with something you built.**

Not a certificate. Not a completion badge. A deployed service, a set of specs and ADRs you wrote, and the experience of having Claude take you from a requirement to validated, running code faster than you have ever moved before.
